Electrical Engineering

History

The Department of Electrical Engineering, as part of the Hasanuddin University Faculty of Engineering (FT) (UNHAS) Makassar, was established in 1963. At that time the UNHAS campus was still located in Baraya. The main mission of the establishment of the Electrical Engineering Department at that time was to provide opportunities for the sons and daughters from the region to study higher education in order to meet the needs of Electrical Engineering experts.


At first the Electrical Engineering Department curriculum was only a single curriculum with several limited elective subjects. This curriculum has undergone various changes and developments since 1963, including the transition from the Undergraduate Program, the 6-year Bachelor Program, to the current Strata and Diploma Program. In 1980, namely at the age of 17, due to the insistence of employment and students, the Electrical Engineering Department curriculum was expanded into 2 (two) sub-programs of study, namely the Sub-Power Engineering Study Program (TTL) and Telecommunications and Electronics Engineering (TTE )


In Tofu 1985, the Faculty of Engineering at UNHAS moved to Tamalanrea. The Electrical Engineering Department occupies the 3rd floor of the Faculty of Engineering building and part of the Heavy Laboratory building. At the same time, the procurement of new laboratory equipment is carried out. For ten years from 1986 to 1996, the Department of Electrical Engineering FT-UNHAS together with the Department of Mechanical Engineering got the Engineer Acceleration Project (PPI) which aims to increase graduation productivity.


In accordance with the provisions of the National Consortium as outlined in the 1995 curriculum, then in 1995 (then slightly modified in 1997) the Electrical Engineering Study Program experienced another division into 3 (three) study programs, namely:

  1. Sub-Energy Engineering Study Program (TE)
  2. Telecommunications Engineering Study Program (TT)
  3. Sub-Study Program in Computer Engineering, Control and Electronics (TK)

Every 5 (five) years since 1995, the Electrical Engineering Study Program of the Department of Electrical Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, Hasanuddin University, Makassar always conducts curriculum revisions. The curriculum currently used is the 2010 Curriculum which is compiled in the format of Competency Based Curriculum (KBK). In the 2015 Curriculum, the Competency-based Curriculum (KBK) has been developed into a Research and Development Based Curriculum (R & D Based Curriculum) or Research and Development (R & D) -based Curriculum in accordance with the LBE (Laboratory-based Education) concept developed by the Faculty of Engineering, Hasanuddin University since the construction of the New Campus on the former land of the Gowa PKG.

Vision

The center of excellence in education, research, and the application of electrical engineering in the fields of energy, control, electronics, computers, telecommunications and information based on the Indonesian Maritime Continent in 2025

Mission

  1. Produce graduates who have good attitudes and values, have general skills, special skills and knowledge in the field of electro and are able to develop successful knowledge independently, creatively, and innovatively.
  2. Producing scientific works in the fields of electro-based energy, control, computers and telecommunications based on the Indonesian Maritime Continent, published nationally and internationally
  3. Disseminating efficient technology for the community that supports improving the quality of life of the community
  4. Establish and strengthen partnerships with alumni, industry, government, community institutions, and related institutions both nationally and internationally to support the improvement of the quality of relevance in teaching, research, and graduate competencies
  5. Manage and develop the Electrical Engineering Study Program by adhering to the principles of accountability, transparency, responsibility, effectiveness, and efficiency

Goal

  1. The availability of human resources in the electrical field who have the ability to compete to get employment or create jobs independently and creatively
  2. The creation of an academic atmosphere that supports increased knowledge in the fields of Energy, Control, Computer, Electronics, Telecommunications and Information Engineering based on the Indonesian Maritime Continent
